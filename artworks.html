Artworks

The artworks at MoMA span some of the most influential movements in modern and contemporary art, from late 19th-century Impressionism to cutting-edge installations and digital media. Visitors can experience iconic masterpieces like Van Gogh’s The Starry Night, Picasso’s Les Demoiselles d’Avignon, and Pollock’s One: Number 31, 1950. MoMA’s collection celebrates innovation, bold expression, and the evolving ideas that have shaped art over the past 140 years.

Collection 1880s - 1940s

MoMA’s 1880s–1940s collection captures the rise of modern art through bold experimentation and new visual languages. Key works like The Starry Night and Les Demoiselles d’Avignon reflect the era’s groundbreaking shift in artistic expression.

Collection 1950s - 1970s

MoMA’s 1950s–1970s collection highlights the explosive creativity of postwar art, from the emotional intensity of Abstract Expressionism to the bold imagery of Pop Art. This era features iconic works by artists like Pollock, Rothko, Warhol, and Lichtenstein who redefined what art could be.

Collection 1980s - Present

​MoMA’s 1980s–Present collection reflects the dynamic evolution of contemporary art, addressing themes such as identity, technology, and social change. Through diverse mediums and global perspectives, artists explore the complexities of the modern world, offering fresh insights into the human experience.
